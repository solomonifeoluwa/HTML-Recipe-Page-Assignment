Classic Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and a rich blend of spices. It’s perfect for family gatherings, parties, and Sunday dinners. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to make authentic smoky Jollof Rice just like we do back home.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes

Cook time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 5 large tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • Salt and seasoning cubes to taste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, peppers, and half the onions into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a pot, add chopped onions, and fry until fragrant.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for 2 minutes, then add blended mixture. Cook for 10–15 minutes until thickened.
  4. Season with curry, thyme, salt, and seasoning cubes. Stir in chicken stock.
  5. Add washed rice, stir well, cover, and cook on low heat until done.
  6. Fluff rice with a fork and serve with grilled chicken or fried plantains.

Tips & Notes

For a smoky flavor, allow the rice at the bottom of the pot to slightly burn — this is the secret to that signature party Jollof taste.
